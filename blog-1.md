# The Four Pillars of OOP in TypeScript

When I first heard "four pillars of OOP" — honestly, it sounded scary. But once I actually used them in code, it clicked. and now its fine to me , xD !!

---

**Encapsulation** is just hiding your data. Don't let other parts of your code touch things they shouldn't. Use `private` and let methods to do the work...

**Inheritance** is reusing code. If two classes share the same logic, put it in a parent class and `extend` it. Stop copy-pasting... saving time and your code is nice to look even ...

**Abstraction** is about keeping things simple for the caller. You don't need to know _how_ the engine works to drive a car. Same idea — expose only what's needed, hide the rest.

**Polymorphism** is one method, different behaviours. A `Dog` and a `Cat` both have `makeSound()` — but they do different things. You call the same method and it just works....

---

That's really it.

These four ideas help me to write code that's easier to read, easier to fix, and easier to grow. You don't need to use all four at once — just start with one and you'll naturally reach for the others. thats it ^ ^
